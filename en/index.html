About

least (ecology and art laboratory for a society in transition) is an association that supports artistic, scientific, ecological, social and participatory approaches in order to activate the collective consciousness and resilience of our “Earth system”: we believe that everything, starting with our practices, our jobs, our habits and our entire daily life, needs to be reappraised and recreated. To do so, least offers new tools to replace the usual processes and contributes to the development of collective thought and action that will change the world.

transdisciplinarity

least brings together scientists, artists, thinkers and local experts to share knowledge and know-how and foster new, long-term positive strategies through different skills and tools.

long term perspective

least is committed to spreading an ecology of production that puts research and knowledge transmission at the centre: a process that requires time and fluidity, far from usual production.

co-creation

least claims the right to champion a form of art without an audience, preferring the notion of a “participating community” to that of a “receiving audience”. Everyone becomes an actor in this form of “art by infiltration”.

Eight people are behind least, its conception, mission, programme and operation.

Together, they decide on calls for projects, develop shared thinking on agreed themes, and accompany the implementation and development of each project. They appoint staff.

Vincent Barras

Director, Institute for medical humanities, and professor in the history of medicine and science UNIL-CHUV (IHM), performer, writer.

Tony Berrada

Professor of finance, UNIGE; director, Geneva Finance Research Institute.

Claire de Ribaupierre

Doctor in letters, specialised in anthropology, dramatist, co-creator of artistic projects.

Alexander Federau

Doctor of philosophy in the field of ecology; diploma in physics, EPFZ, active for many years on environmental issues.

Rodrigo Fernandez

Doctor in materials engineering EPFL; co-founder of Terrabloc (winner of the Prix Sud 2019, sustainable start-up).

Véronique Ferrero Delacoste

Curator specialising in the performing arts; Masters degree in Art in the public sphere, director of artistic, participative and co-creative projects.

Adina Secretan

Choreographer and director; Masters degree in philosophy, responsible for social and community arts projects.

Laurent de Wurstemberger

Architect AAM; founder of the architects’ studio LDW; co-founder of Terrabloc (winner of the Prix Sud 2019, sustainable start-up).
